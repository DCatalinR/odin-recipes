Carbonara

Carbonara is a sumptuous Italian pasta dish known for its velvety, creamy sauce enveloping perfectly cooked spaghetti. The sauce, rich in flavor, complements the pasta, offering a delightful and indulgent experience that captures the essence of Roman culinary tradition.

Ingredients per 1 person

  • 100 grams of Spaghetti
  • 70 grams of Guanciale
  • 1 Egg
  • 40 grams of grated Pecorino Romano
  • 40 grams of grated Parmesan cheese
  • Freshly grounded black pepper
  • Salt for the pasta water
  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, whisk together the eggs, Pecorino Romano, and Parmesan cheese in a bowl. Season with a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper.
  3. In a separate pan, cook the diced pancetta or guanciale over medium heat until it becomes crispy. Remove from heat.
  4. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta water before draining the cooked spaghetti.
  5. Immediately after draining, add the hot spaghetti to the pan with the cooked pancetta or guanciale. Toss to coat the pasta evenly with the rendered fat.
  6. Pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, quickly tossing to combine. The heat from the pasta will cook the eggs and create a creamy sauce. If needed, add reserved pasta water gradually to reach your desired consistency.
  7. Plate the carbonara immediately, ensuring it's piping hot. Garnish with extra Pecorino Romano, Parmesan, and black pepper.
  8. Dive in and savour the rich, creamy goodness of your homemade traditional carbonara!

Remember, traditional carbonara is simple yet exquisite, so let the quality of your ingredients shine through. Buon appetito!
